My name is Marc-Anthony Jones and I attend Fayetteville Technical Community College (FTCC). Attending a trade school has become one of the best decisions I have ever made. I have learned so much about myself, my life, and my future plans while attending FTCC. I graduated from Shenandoah Valley Academy in July of 2020. Since then I have become very active in my everyday task as an adult. I work a part time job, I exercise regularly, and you guessed it, attend a community college. However it has been a bumpy road since attending FTCC. I failed two classes, almost dropped out of college after 1 semester, and I was starting to doubt if I had picked the right major. But with time, prayer, planning, and perseverance I have become one of the top students in my major. I study Information Technology PC Support & Services. Fast forwarded 2 years since my first semester, I've made deans list multiple times for academic achievements, I have received an academic scholarship, my confidence in myself is at an all time high, and I am continuing to grow in many other ways.

Plans After Community College

All great things must come to an end. I will graduate from FTCC in May of 2023 with an Associates Degree in IT Support. From there I will take the CompTIA A+ Certification Exam and transfer to Oakwood University to obtain my Bachlor's Degree. I also plan to start an Internship with a coporate office or work at the schools IT department to gain experience. Once all that has been completed, I will begin my IT Career.
